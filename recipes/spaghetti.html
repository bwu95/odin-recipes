Spaghetti Recipe

Mouthwatering Spaghetti Recipe

Meatball spaghetti is a classic dish that has been enjoyed for generations. To make this comforting and flavorful meal, you will need to start by making your meatballs. Combine ground beef, breadcrumbs, grated Parmesan cheese, finely chopped onion, minced garlic, an egg, and some salt and pepper in a large mixing bowl. Use your hands to mix the ingredients together and form small meatballs. Next, cook your spaghetti according to the package instructions and set it aside. In a large saucepan, heat some olive oil and sauté some chopped onion and garlic until they are soft and fragrant. Add a can of crushed tomatoes, some dried basil and oregano, and some salt and pepper. Let the sauce simmer for a few minutes before adding the meatballs. Cover the pan and let the meatballs cook in the sauce for about 15-20 minutes, or until they are cooked through. Serve the meatballs and sauce over the cooked spaghetti and garnish with fresh parsley and grated Parmesan cheese. This dish is sure to be a hit with your family or guests, and it's perfect for a cozy weeknight dinner or a special occasion.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound (450 g) ground beef
  • 1/2 cup (45 g) breadcrumbs
  • 1/2 cup (50 g) grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 egg
  • 1 pound (450 g) spaghetti
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can (28 oz / 800 g) crushed tomatoes
  • Chopped fresh parsley, for garnish (optional)

Cooking Step Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground beef, breadcrumbs, grated Parmesan cheese, finely chopped onion, minced garlic, egg, salt, and black pepper. Mix well with your hands until all ingredients are well combined.
  2. Use your hands to form the mixture into small meatballs, about 1 inch (2.5 cm) in diameter.
  3. Heat the olive oil in a large saucepan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic and sauté for 2-3 minutes until the onion is translucent.
  4. Add the crushed tomatoes, dried basil, dried oregano, salt, and black pepper to the saucepan and stir well.
  5. Gently place the meatballs into the tomato sauce and stir until all meatballs are coated with the sauce.
  6. Cover the saucepan with a lid and let the meatballs cook in the tomato sauce for 15-20 minutes, or until they are cooked through.
  7. In a separate pot, cook the spaghetti according to the package instructions until al dente.
  8. Drain the spaghetti and add it to the saucepan with the meatballs and tomato sauce.
  9. Gently toss the spaghetti with the sauce and meatballs until the spaghetti is well coated.
  10. Serve the spaghetti and meatballs hot, garnished with chopped fresh parsley and grated Parmesan cheese if desired.
